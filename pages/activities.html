Women@SCS

Women@SCS is Carnegie Mellon's organization to support and promote women in Computer Science. The organization is involved in organizing mentorship for and promoting a sense of community among undergraduate and graduate women in Computer Science as well as in Computer Science outreach to various schools in the area.
My efforts within Women@SCS include:
Undergraduate Sisters Program
I'm a co-chair for this program. Here's what I do in this capacity:
I help manage the matching process for Big/Little Sisters, where freshmen women are assigned an upperclass mentor whom they can easily approach. As part of this, in Fall 2013, I have improved the process, so that everyone involved submits preferences which we compile into a cheat sheet. Then we hold an event where, with the aid of the cheat sheet, prospective big and little sisters can approach each other and conduct a meaningful conversation. We then take rankings and preferences from everyone involved and analyze them to make the matchings
I organize various social events both on and off campus where undergrad women can join in to do a fun activity while munching goodies and chatting with others
Webteam
I'm part of a team that manages the website for Women@SCS. Our efforts include:
Redesigning various parts of the website using modern technologies, while ensuring support for older content dating as far back as 1999
Updating the content of the website to reflect the ongoing activities of the organization

The Tartan

The Tartan is Carnegie Mellon's weekly student-run newspaper.
Here's what I contribute:
Copy Editor
I'm part of a team that reads through the pages of the newspaper to identify and fix any errors to ensure that articles preserve integrity of content and conform to our established style. As part of this, I scan for spelling and grammatical errors, fix layout as well as ensure that names are spelt right and sources are cited correctly.
